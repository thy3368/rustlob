# Delegation Program

## Overview

Validators are essential to Hyperliquid's integrity and efficiency. The Hyper Foundation Delegation Program aims to:

* Enhance network security by delegating tokens to reliable, high-performing validators
* Promote diversity across the validator network to strengthen decentralization
* Support validators committed to Hyperliquid ecosystem growth and stability

Testnet performance serves as a criterion for mainnet delegation when mainnet metrics aren't available. The Foundation monitors delegations continuously and reserves the right to cease delegation anytime.

Foundation validators will strongly consider Delegation Program participation when evaluating peer validators. Those planning to run mainnet validators are encouraged to apply before setup.

## Eligibility

* You must hold 10k HYPE in a single address before applying, with a minimum 10k HYPE self-delegation locked for one year
* You must operate at least two non-validator nodes with 95% uptime if accepted. These nodes' IP addresses will be publicly shared and attributed to you. Others will use your nodes as seed nodes. IPs must be static (e.g., AWS elastic IPs). Do not expose non-validator ports publicly until an official announcement. Never open validator ports publicly
* You must comply with applicable laws and regulations
* You must complete KYC/KYB processes successfully
* You cannot be from restricted jurisdictions including "Ontario, the U.S., Cuba, Iran, Myanmar, North Korea, Syria, and certain Russian-occupied regions of Ukraine"
* The Foundation may adjust eligibility criteria at any time
